MORMON PIONEERS

Journey of Faith & Sacrifice

1846 - 1869

THE JOURNEY

FEBRUARY 1846

The Exodus Begins

The first wagons leave Nauvoo, Illinois, as Latter-day Saints flee persecution. Over 70,000 pioneers will make the journey over the next 23 years.

1846-1847

Winter Quarters

Thousands winter at Council Bluffs, Iowa (then called Winter Quarters). Some stay for years. Many die from illness and exposure.

JULY 21, 1847

Entering the Valley

Brigham Young and the first company arrive in Salt Lake Valley. Young declares, "This is the right place."

JULY 24, 1847

Pioneer Day

The arrival is celebrated. Today, Pioneer Day is a Utah state holiday commemorating this historic journey.

1856-1860

Handcart Companies

Over 3,000 pioneers pull handcarts across the plains. The Martin and Willie handcart companies face tragic rescues in 1856.

THE TRAIL

1846

Nauvoo, Illinois

Starting point. The temple is completed and dedicated. Saints are forced to leave after intense persecution.

1846

Garden Grove, Iowa

First major camp. Pioneers plant crops and build cabins to help later companies.

Winter 1846

Council Bluffs, Iowa (Winter Quarters)

Main outfitting point. Up to 4,000 Saints winter here. Many die from disease and exposure.

Summer 1847

Fort Laramie, Wyoming

Trading post and resupply point. About 1/3 of the journey complete.

July 1847

Independence Rock, Wyoming

Natural landmark where pioneers carved names and dates. Last chance watering point before the mountains.

July 1847

Sweetwater River, Wyoming

Dangerous river crossings. Several pioneers drown. The Devil's Gate is a narrow canyon pass.

July 1847

Echo Canyon, Utah

Final mountain approach. Steep walls. Engineers blow up cliffs to widen the trail.

July 21, 1847

Salt Lake Valley, Utah

Destination! Desolate desert that becomes a thriving settlement. Pioneers start irrigation and building.

WILLIE & MARTIN COMPANIES

April 14, 1847

"We have had a very cold time of it since we left Winter Quarters. The snow is quite deep and the wind blows terribly. We are now on the Sweetwater, the water is very cold."

— William Clayton, Pioneer Journal

July 21, 1847

"This morning we started very early... We traveled about 2 miles and came in sight of the valley... The valley looks green and the mountains are covered with snow."

— Orson Pratt, Pioneer Journal

June 28, 1856

"Last night was the coldest we have had... Several of our company gave out today. Their feet are badly frozen... I feel to thank God in my soul for his mercies to us."

— John Chislett, Martin Handcart Company

October 1856

"We have been obliged to lay by two days on account of the snow... There are about fifty of us that are not able to walk... I feel confident that if we had a little help we could get through."

— Willie Handcart Company survivor

May 1849

"We are now on the Missouri River, about 600 miles from Winter Quarters. The weather is very pleasant... We have plenty of buffalo meat and are in good health."

— Parley P. Pratt, Autobiography

July 24, 1847

"This morning we arrived in the Great Salt Lake Valley... We pitched our tents and gave thanks to God for his guiding hand. We are now in the promised land."

— Heber C. Kimball, Pioneer Journal
